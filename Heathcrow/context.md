# Heathcrow Campaign Context

Use this file for campaign history and prior decisions. Use [Daisy's current
character sheet](Daisy-character-sheet-20260712.md) for current mechanics; it

## Rules and source priority

- The campaign uses **2014 D&D 5e**.
- Splendor is the campaign setting. DM-approved material from *Steinhardt's
  Guide to the Eldritch Hunt* includes Daisy's Osteomancer subclass, Worthless
  Husk background, osteomancy spells, and Osteomantic Adept feat.
- DM rulings override published rules. Older chat answers are research notes,
  not authority.
- The generated Veyrhold material is a **creative proposal**, not established
  campaign canon unless the DM adopts it.

## Daisy's established backstory

These details came directly from the player's answers:

- Daisy uses he/him pronouns, is male, and is about 17; trauma makes his exact
  age uncertain.
- Daisy is his birth name and one of his few memories of his parents.
- He is an albino/white Kenku from a very poor, crime-ridden place in Heathcrow.
  His parents were murdered when he was young, and he survived alone through
  theft and street life.
- He had a best friend who died when Daisy was eight. The friend's identity and
  death were not established by the player.
- Daisy learned magic alone from found and stolen spellbooks. He chose
  osteomancy after being mocked as a hollow-boned bird. His first bone spell was
  Phalangeal Shot, which he first used to kill a tormentor.
- Daisy's osteomancy is deliberate, violent, and surgical. Bone eruptions leave
  permanent featherless scars.
- The scar across his eyes came from escaping someone who was cutting into his
  face and skull; Daisy killed the attacker with bone magic.
- He is observant, quiet, blunt, mistrustful, acquisitive, and survival-focused.
  He fears the unknown and betrayal, distrusts kindness, and constantly checks
  behind himself.
- He values his mind, knowledge, independence, perseverance, money, strength,
  and safety. He sees corpses and skeletons as tools, evidence, and resources.
- He has a selective soft spot for discarded people but is still selfish, cruel,
  and morally numb. His intended arc is toward limited humanity rather than
  uncomplicated goodness.
- He began the campaign by awakening restrained in the sacrificial dungeon. He
  views the party as some mixture of shield, tool, liability, and temporary
  arrangement.
- He was beginning to trust Amita, a small warrior child and thief, and watched
  Stumpy, the tree monk, carefully. Seeing a party member eat someone alive
  affected him.
- He wants eventually to return home for money, safety, unresolved rivals and
  debts, stolen-book trails, and answers about his parents.

## Story-relevant items and hooks

- The Mithral Plate Cloak came from a mimic chest.
- The occult spellbook came from a dungeon room and granted three DM-approved
  osteomancy spells.
- In the first session, Daisy found a diary with a severed finger attached.
  Calcified Memories showed its owner being hunted near death and suggested the
  diary belonged to someone who ran a city.
- Daisy took a gold skeleton leg after the party killed a gold skeleton and
  intended to sell it.
- Rare herbal tea and Charles Gray's necklace were also treated as valuables to
  sell.
- The starting key to an important location remains undefined.
- The exact deaths of Daisy's parents and childhood friend, the skull-cutter's
  identity, the dungeon abduction, the diary and finger, and Daisy's eventual
  return home remain available DM hooks.
