# D&D Research Sources

Use this list when researching rules, characters, or campaign details. Prefer
official sources, and always check which version of the rules a campaign uses.

## Rules versions

D&D Beyond now labels the original 2014 fifth-edition rules as **5e** and the
revised 2024 rules as **5.5e**. They are compatible, but many details differ.
Record the version used by each campaign and do not mix versions without saying
so.

## Primary sources

- [D&D Beyond Basic Rules][basic-2024]
  — Official free 2024/5.5e rules for players and DMs, including classes,
  spells, equipment, monsters, and the rules glossary.
- [Basic Rules (2014)][basic-2014]
  — Official free rules for campaigns using the original 2014/5e rules.
- [D&D Beyond classes][classes], [spells][spells], and [monsters][monsters]
  — Useful searchable indexes. Check each result's rules version and source
  because these pages mix official, partnered, third-party, free, and paid
  content.
- [System Reference Document][srd]
  — Official downloadable rules. Use SRD 5.1 for 2014/5e or SRD 5.2.1 for
  2024/5.5e. SRD material is reusable under CC BY 4.0 with attribution, but it
  does not contain every option from the books.
- [Sage Advice and Errata][sage-advice]
  — Official corrections and rulings for unclear rules. Make sure a ruling or
  correction matches the campaign's rules version.
- [D&D character sheets][character-sheets] and [D&D Beyond campaigns][campaigns]
  — Official tools for character records and campaign sharing. Accounts or
  purchased content may be required for some features.

When a campaign uses a purchased sourcebook or adventure, that book and its
errata should be treated as the primary source for its content.

## Secondary sources

- [D&D 5e Wikidot][wikidot]
  — Convenient for quick lookups, especially for 2014 material. It also
  contains setting-specific, playtest, and homebrew content, so verify important
  details against an official source before recording them.
- [Forgotten Realms Wiki][forgotten-realms]
  — Helpful for Forgotten Realms lore and for locating the books where a detail
  originated. It covers multiple editions, so check article citations and
  dates.
- [RPG Stack Exchange: D&D 5e][rpg-stack-exchange]
  — Helpful for finding explanations of difficult rules interactions. Answers
  are community interpretations, not official rulings.

## Recording research

When adding researched information to this repository:

1. Link to the source or record the book title and page number.
2. State whether it uses 2014/5e or 2024/5.5e rules.
3. Label unofficial material as **homebrew**, **playtest**, or **community
   interpretation**.
4. Keep campaign facts and house rules separate from published rules.
5. Summarize what is needed instead of copying large sections of copyrighted
   books or websites.
